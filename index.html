Brand Vigilance Workshop

By Krishna Rathi

Dear Founders,

I know you’ve already built a brand.

It looks right.
Logo, packaging, story—all in place.

But let me say this to you directly.

What you’re calling “building a brand”...
I’ve seen founders do the same and still lose it.

— A founder who built a strong hospitality brand like StayZilla, but couldn’t hold it when things turned.

— Founders behind Paper Boat, who had to deal with trademark conflicts even after building recall.

— Even a company like Cognizant, facing trademark restrictions in certain markets despite its scale.

Not because they didn’t build well.
Because they didn’t secure what they built.

And that’s the gap.

You’re not just building perception.
You’re building an asset.

If you don’t own it cleanly,
it’s not an asset.

It’s a risk sitting inside your business.

• • •

From the moment you approve your logo
to the point you start scaling,

you need to be clear on:

  • [ ] Is your brand actually safe, or already exposed?
  • [ ] Can you legally scale the name you’re investing in?
  • [ ] Do you really own it or does someone else hold a piece of it?
  • [ ] Are there risks that can stop funding, expansion, or acquisition?
  • [ ] Are your agencies, collaborators, or partners creating silent IP gaps?

This is where most founders realise things late.
And fixing it later is always expensive.

• • •

I’m putting together a small, closed workshop for 30 founders.

If you’re serious about building something you actually own,
you can apply.

- Krishna Rathi
