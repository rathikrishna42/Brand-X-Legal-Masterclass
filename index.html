BUILD BRANDS LEGALLY

A Workshop by Krishna Rathi

Dear Founder,

I know you already understand how to build a brand in an exceptional decorative way.

You understand the importance of a great logo, memorable packaging, brand storytelling narrative;
But let me say this to you directly.

What you’re calling “building a brand”,
is also understood by many founders I meet everyday but still they could not own the brand.

The dairy giant Amul stopping the innerwear brand Amul Macho, proving ‘well-known’ trademarks are beyond regular trademarks that protect your reputation everywhere.

Founders behind Paper Boat, who had to deal with trademark conflicts even after building recall.

Even a company like Cognizant was restrained from using its own logo.

In the lifespan of a brand building cycle, you are building the most important asset i.e., The Brand. An asset without ownership is merely a liability. Building a brand asset must come with the responsibility of protecting the asset.

In my decade of brand building experience, I have been taking this dual responsibility of building and protecting my client’s brand assets before it is too late for them.

• • •

I believe every brand builder should do so.
Right from approving the logo design to announcing your brand’s IPO, you must be solidly clear on:

  • Is your brand legally safe, or silently exposed?
  • Can you legally scale the name you’re investing in?
  • Do you really own it or does someone else hold a piece of it?
  • Are there risks that can stop funding, expansion, or acquisition because of such minor negligence?
  • Are your agencies, collaborators, or partners creating silent IP gaps that can claim ownership after years of gap?

This is where most founders realise things late.
And fixing it later is always an expensive challenge.

• • •

But if you are still reading this, we still have time.
I’m putting together a small, closed workshop for 30 founders.

If you’re serious about building something you actually wish to own, I am happy to host you.
Lets build brand ‘legally’ this time.

Regards,

— Krishna Rathi

Brand Strategist & Lawyer
